Privacy & Data Protection Policy

This website respects the rights of individuals in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Data Collection
This website does not collect, store, or process any personal data of visitors. Browsing the website does not require registration and does not involve the transmission of personal information. The only data subject to processing is the administrator of the platform.

2. Types of Data Processed
The website uses a secure SQLite database that stores the following categories of data:
– Artist’s stage name
– Concert schedules (dates, venues, and related details)
– Tours (cities, countries, itineraries)
– Albums (titles, descriptions, and distribution links)
– Contact details (telephone numbers, email addresses, official social media links)
These items are considered personal or sensitive data under the GDPR insofar as they may identify or be linked to a natural person.

3. Purpose of Processing
Data is processed exclusively for the purpose of publishing artistic content, such as concert information, tours, albums, and contact details. No commercial profiling, targeted marketing, or sale of data is performed.

4. Data Controller
The administrator of the platform is the sole Data Controller and ensures lawful, fair, and transparent processing of all data.

5. Data Retention
Data is retained only as long as necessary for artistic and informational purposes. It may be updated or deleted upon request by the artist or when no longer relevant.

6. Cookies
This website uses cookies to ensure proper functionality:
– Essential cookies: strictly necessary for technical operation (e.g. navigation, secure access). Acceptance of these cookies does not identify or register visitors in any way.
– Technical cookies: support improved browsing and user experience. Acceptance of these cookies has no negative consequence for visitors.
– Non-essential cookies: the website does not use cookies for analytics, marketing, or tracking purposes.
More information on cookies can be found on the official European Commission Cookies Policy.

7. User Rights
Visitors are not subject to any personal data processing under GDPR. The administrator, as the sole data subject, retains all rights granted by GDPR, including access, rectification, erasure, and restriction of processing.

8. Security
Appropriate technical and organizational measures are in place to ensure the integrity and security of the SQLite database. Data is not shared with third parties.

For further details on your rights, please refer to the GDPR Articles 12–23.
